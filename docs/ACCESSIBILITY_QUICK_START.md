# Spectra Accessibility Quick Start Guide

Welcome! This guide will help you get started with Spectra, your AI-powered voice assistant for hands-free computer control.

## What is Spectra?

Spectra is an AI assistant designed specifically for blind and visually impaired users. She helps you navigate websites, read content, and complete tasks using only your voice. Spectra can see your screen and respond to natural language commands.

**Important**: Spectra refers to herself as "Spectra" - she's your personal assistant, not just "an AI".

## Quick Setup (5 Minutes)

### Prerequisites
- Chrome or Edge browser
- Microphone access
- Internet connection
- (Optional) Screen reader software (NVDA, JAWS, or VoiceOver)

### Installation Steps

1. **Install the Spectra Extension**
   - Open Chrome Web Store
   - Search for "Spectra Voice Assistant"
   - Click "Add to Chrome"
   - Grant microphone and screen capture permissions

2. **Open Spectra**
   - Click the Spectra icon in your browser toolbar
   - Or press `Alt+Shift+S` (Windows/Linux) or `Cmd+Shift+S` (Mac)

3. **Start Your First Session**
   - Press `Q` to start Spectra
   - Say "Hello Spectra"
   - Wait for her response

That's it! You're ready to use Spectra.

## Essential Voice Commands

### Getting Oriented

**"Where am I?"**
- Spectra will tell you what website or application you're viewing
- Example response: "You're on Google.com - I can see the search homepage"

**"What do you see?"** or **"Describe the screen"**
- Spectra will describe the current page content
- She'll mention buttons, links, text, and layout

**"What can I do here?"**
- Spectra will suggest available actions based on what's visible

### Navigation Commands

Spectra understands natural language! Use whichever variation feels natural:

**Clicking Elements:**
- "Click the search button"
- "Press the login link"
- "Tap the submit button"
- "Select the first result"

All of these work the same way - use whatever feels natural to you.

**Typing Text:**
- "Type hello world"
- "Enter my email address"
- "Write a message"
- "Input my name"

**Scrolling:**
- "Scroll down"
- "Scroll up"
- "Scroll to the bottom"
- "Go to the top of the page"

**Opening Websites:**
- "Go to Google"
- "Open Gmail"
- "Visit YouTube"
- "Navigate to BBC News"

### Context-Aware Commands

Spectra remembers what you just talked about:

1. "What's the first search result?"
   - Spectra: "The first result is Wikipedia..."
2. "Click it"
   - Spectra will click the Wikipedia link she just mentioned

This works for any element Spectra has recently described.

## Common Workflows

### Searching on Google

1. Say: "Go to Google"
2. Say: "Type best restaurants near me"
3. Say: "Press the search button" (or just "search")
4. Say: "What are the results?"
5. Say: "Click the first result"

### Reading an Article

1. Navigate to any news website
2. Say: "What articles are available?"
3. Say: "Click the article about [topic]"
4. Say: "Read the article" or "What does it say?"
5. Say: "Scroll down" to continue reading

### Checking Email (Gmail)

1. Say: "Go to Gmail"
2. Say: "What emails do I have?"
3. Say: "Open the first email"
4. Say: "Read it to me"
5. Say: "Reply to this email"
6. Say: "Type [your message]"
7. Say: "Send the email"

### Online Shopping

1. Navigate to shopping website
2. Say: "Search for [product name]"
3. Say: "What products are shown?"
4. Say: "Tell me about the first product"
5. Say: "Add it to cart"
6. Say: "Go to checkout"

## Keyboard Shortcuts

While Spectra is voice-first, these keyboard shortcuts are helpful:

- **Q** - Start or stop Spectra
- **W** - Toggle screen sharing on/off
- **Escape** - Stop Spectra immediately
- **Tab** - Navigate between interface elements (works with screen readers)

## Using Spectra with Screen Readers

Spectra works alongside your screen reader (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver):

### Audio Management
- Spectra automatically reduces her volume when your screen reader speaks
- Both audio streams remain audible - no conflicts
- You can navigate with your screen reader while Spectra responds

### Best Practices
1. **Start your screen reader first**, then start Spectra
2. **Use screen reader for interface navigation** (buttons, menus)
3. **Use Spectra for web content** (reading articles, describing pages)
4. **They complement each other** - use both together!

### Keyboard Navigation
- All Spectra features work with keyboard only
- No mouse required
- Tab through interface elements
- Screen reader announces all status changes

## Understanding Spectra's Responses

### Location Queries
When you ask "Where am I?", Spectra analyses your screen and tells you:
- The website name (e.g., "You're on Google.com")
- The application (e.g., "You're in Gmail")
- The page context (e.g., "You're on the search results page")

She will **never** tell you your physical GPS location - she's talking about your digital location.

### Screen Descriptions
Spectra describes what she actually sees on your screen:
- Visible text and headings
- Buttons and links
- Images and their context
- Page layout and structure
- Interactive elements

### Identity
Spectra always refers to herself as "Spectra":
- "I'm Spectra, and I can help you with that"
- "I can see the page you're on"
- "Let me describe what I see"

She won't say "As an AI" - she's Spectra, your assistant.

## Troubleshooting

### Spectra Isn't Responding

**Check the status indicator:**
- Green "Listening..." = Spectra is ready
- Red "Disconnected" = Connection issue

**Solutions:**
1. Press `Q` to restart Spectra
2. Check your internet connection
3. Refresh the browser page
4. Check microphone permissions in browser settings

### Spectra Can't See Your Screen

**Symptoms:**
- Spectra says "I cannot see your screen"
- Screen descriptions are generic or unhelpful

**Solutions:**
1. Press `W` to enable screen sharing
2. Grant screen capture permission when prompted
3. Select the correct window/screen to share
4. Verify "Screen sharing: on" status

### Spectra Doesn't Understand Commands

**Tips for better recognition:**
- Speak clearly and at normal pace
- Use natural language - don't use robotic commands
- Try command variations (click/press/tap all work)
- Be specific: "Click the blue login button" vs "click button"

### Vision System Errors

If you see error messages like:
- "Vision analysis failed: Invalid API key"
- "Vision analysis timed out"
- "Rate limit exceeded"

**Solutions:**
1. Wait a moment and try again
2. Check your internet connection
3. Contact support if errors persist

### Audio Conflicts with Screen Reader

**If you can't hear your screen reader:**
1. Check screen reader volume settings
2. Verify audio ducking is enabled in Spectra settings
3. Try stopping Spectra (`Q`), then restarting

**If you can't hear Spectra:**
1. Check browser audio permissions
2. Verify system volume is not muted
3. Check Spectra volume settings

## Tips for Success

### 1. Be Conversational
Talk to Spectra naturally:
- ✅ "Can you click the login button for me?"
- ✅ "What's on this page?"
- ✅ "Help me find the search box"

### 2. Be Specific When Needed
If there are multiple similar elements:
- ✅ "Click the blue submit button"
- ✅ "Select the first search result"
- ✅ "Type in the email field"

### 3. Use Context
Spectra remembers recent conversation:
- You: "What's the first article?"
- Spectra: "The first article is about climate change..."
- You: "Read it" ← Spectra knows what "it" refers to

### 4. Ask for Help
If you're unsure what to do:
- "What can I do here?"
- "What options are available?"
- "Help me navigate this page"

### 5. Confirm Important Actions
For critical actions, Spectra will ask for confirmation:
- "Are you sure you want to delete this?"
- "Should I proceed with the purchase?"

## Privacy and Security

### What Spectra Can See
- Your screen content when screen sharing is enabled
- Websites you visit while using Spectra
- Text you ask her to type

### What Spectra Cannot See
- Content on other screens or windows (unless shared)
- Your screen when screen sharing is off
- Other applications when browser is not in focus

### Your Control
- Press `W` to turn screen sharing on/off anytime
- Press `Q` to stop Spectra completely
- Close the browser tab to end the session
- All processing happens in real-time - nothing is permanently stored

### Common Questions

**Q: Can Spectra work offline?**
A: No, Spectra requires an internet connection for voice processing and vision analysis.

**Q: Does Spectra work on mobile devices?**
A: Currently, Spectra is optimised for desktop browsers (Chrome, Edge).

**Q: Can I use Spectra in languages other than English?**
A: Currently, Spectra primarily supports English. Additional language support is planned.

**Q: How accurate is Spectra's vision system?**
A: Spectra's vision system is highly accurate for standard web content. Complex layouts or unusual designs may require more specific commands.

**Q: Can Spectra fill out forms automatically?**
A: Yes! Spectra can identify form fields and enter data. Just tell her what to type in each field.
